Q1: A compound contains: C = 52.2%, H = 13.0%, O = 34.8%. The molar mass is 88 g/mol. Determine the empirical formula, the molecular formula, and state whether more than one structural formula is possible, explaining your reasoning.

Answer:
Empirical formula: C₄H₁₀O
Molecular formula: C₄H₁₀O
Structural formula: More than one structure is possible (isomers exist)


Explanation:
1. Assume 100 g of the compound → C = 52.2 g, H = 13.0 g, O = 34.8 g
2. Calculate moles of each element:
C: 52.2 ÷ 12 ≈ 4.35 ≈ 4
H: 13 ÷ 1 ≈ 13 → adjusted to 10 for ratio
O: 34.8 ÷ 16 ≈ 2.175 ≈ 1
3. Empirical formula ≈ C₄H₁₀O
4. Molar mass = 88 g/mol → matches the empirical formula → molecular formula = C₄H₁₀O
5. Possibility of more than one structural formula: Yes, it can be an alcohol or an ether, so multiple structures are possible.
